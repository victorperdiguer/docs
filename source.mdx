The Source step is where you tell Uxia what your testers should navigate. You have two options:

- Point testers at something live.
- Upload your own media and build the flow yourself.

## Live source

Test a live website or a live prototype by entering its URL. Compatible with Figma Make, Figma prototypes, Lovable, Base44, Google Stitch, Claude, and any browser-accessible site.

- **URL**: paste a valid public URL (`www.example.com` or `https://example.com`). Uxia fetches a snapshot and verifies compatibility automatically, usually within a few seconds. Testers navigate the exact URL you enter.
- **Viewport**: choose the screen size testers experience: Desktop (1920×1080), Laptop (1366×768), Tablet (1024×768), Mobile (390×844), or a custom size.

Gated and authenticated products work too, you provide credentials later, in [Advanced Settings](/advanced-settings).

<Note>
  Some websites have anti-robot measures in place. If you encounter one of these cases, contact us, and we will provide instructions for your IT team on how to whitelist our testers so they can navigate your experience.
</Note>

## Media source

When you're testing static screens or a recorded walkthrough rather than a live, interactive product, upload images or a video and set up the flow yourself. You do this on the [media canvas](/media-canvas), where you arrange screens, create the interaction areas and define how testers move between them.

## What you can test

- Live websites and web applications - public, gated with credentials, staging, or pre-production
- Prototypes and generated apps from Figma Make, Figma prototypes, Lovable, Base44, Google Stitch, and Claude
- Static designs, image sets, and video walkthroughs
- Complex multi-step and branched flows
