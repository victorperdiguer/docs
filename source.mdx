The Source step is where you tell Uxia what your testers should navigate. You have two options: point testers at something live, or upload your own media and build the flow yourself.

## Live source

Test a live website or a live prototype by entering its URL. Compatible with Figma Make, Figma prototypes, Lovable, Base44, Google Stitch, Claude, and any browser-accessible site.

- **URL** — paste a valid public URL (`www.example.com` or `https://example.com`). Uxia fetches a snapshot and verifies compatibility automatically, usually within a few seconds. Testers navigate the exact URL you enter.
- **Viewport** — choose the screen size testers experience: Desktop (1920×1080), Laptop (1366×768), Tablet (1024×768), Mobile (390×844), or a custom size.

Gated and authenticated products work too — you provide credentials later, in [Advanced Settings](/advanced-settings).

## Media source

When you're testing static screens or a recorded walkthrough rather than a live, interactive product, upload images or a video and set up the flow yourself. You do this on the [media canvas](/media-canvas), where you arrange screens and define how testers move between them.

## What you can test

- Live websites and web applications — public, staging, or pre-production
- Prototypes and generated apps from Figma Make, Figma prototypes, Lovable, Base44, Google Stitch, and Claude
- Gated products — provide credentials to unlock authenticated flows
- Static designs, image sets, and video walkthroughs
- Complex multi-step flows, and partially built or in-progress products
