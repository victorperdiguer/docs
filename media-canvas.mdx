title: "Media canvas"
---

The **media canvas** is where you build a test flow from your own media instead of pointing testers at a live URL. Upload your screens or a recorded walkthrough, arrange them, draw the interactive areas and define how testers move between them - Uxia turns that into a navigable prototype the AI testers can explore.

## When to use it

Reach for the media canvas when you're testing:

- Static design screens exported from Figma, Sketch, or any design tool (JPG, PNG, or PDF)
- An image set representing a flow that isn't built yet
- A recorded video walkthrough of a product or concept
- Very early iterations of designs where you haven't built a prototype

It's the counterpart to a [live source](/source): the same test, the same missions and testers, but you supply and wire up the screens yourself rather than having Uxia navigate a live site.

## Building the flow

On the canvas, you lay out your screens and connect them into the path you want testers to follow - defining which screen leads to the next so testers' actions move them through the flow the way a real interface would. Once the flow is set, the rest of the builder - [mission](/mission), [scenario](/test-scenario) and [audience](/audiences/overview) - work exactly as it does for a live test.

## Canvas Controls

### Click Mode

- **Enabled:** Drag screens to reposition them.
- **Disabled:** Drag to move around the canvas.

### Create Interaction Areas

Draw interactive regions on a screen and connect them to the next step in your flow.

### Import Files

Upload additional screens without leaving the canvas.

### Fit All Screens

Automatically adjust the view to display every screen on the canvas.

### Zoom In

Zoom in for a closer view using the **\+** button, mouse wheel, or trackpad.

### Zoom Out

Zoom out to see more of your prototype at once.

### Pan

Move around the canvas by dragging, using your trackpad, mouse wheel, or holding the **Spacebar**.

### Help

Open this guide to learn about the available canvas tools.

## Actions

### Click

Use for buttons, links, or any element that navigates to another screen after being clicked.

### Input

Use for text fields or search bars to simulate typing before moving to the next screen.

### Scroll & Swipe

Use for long pages, carousels, or swipeable content. Supports vertical and horizontal movement.

### Drag

Use when the user must drag an element, such as a slider or draggable object.

## Understanding Screens & Connections

Each uploaded image is a screen. The navigation flow is defined by the interaction areas and their connections - not by the screen numbers.

When opening a step from a transcript, the canvas automatically centers on the corresponding screen so you can understand what the tester was seeing.

## How do I create a full-screen scroll?

Create an interaction area covering the entire screen, assign it the **Scroll & Swipe** action, and connect it to the next screen showing the scrolled state.

## How do I simulate text input?

Create an **Input** interaction over the empty field and connect it to another screen where the field appears completed.

For forms with multiple fields, you can either complete them all in one step or create separate screens for a more realistic experience.

## Do testers experience transition effects?

No. Transition animations shown in the Preview are only for building and reviewing the prototype. Testers only experience the interactions and resulting screens.
