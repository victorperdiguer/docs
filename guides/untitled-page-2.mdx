Uxia is available directly in the ChatGPT [plugin directory](https://chatgpt.com/plugins) — approved by OpenAI for distribution as both a plugin and an MCP connector. That means you can connect it in a couple of clicks, with no developer mode or manual server URL required.

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the directory">
    In ChatGPT, go to **Settings → Apps → Directory**, or open [chatgpt.com/plugins](https://chatgpt.com/plugins) directly, and search for **Uxia**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Uxia">
    Open the Uxia listing and click **Connect**. ChatGPT will bring up an authorization dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authenticate">
    Sign in with your Uxia account to authorize the connection. Once approved, Uxia is linked to your ChatGPT account with scoped access to your tests, transcripts, and insights.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  MCP access is included on all paid Uxia plans. If your workspace is still on the Free Trial, upgrade from your [Plans & Credits](/account/plans-and-credits) page before connecting.
</Note>

<Note>
  If your ChatGPT workspace is on a Business, Enterprise, or Edu plan, an admin or owner may need to enable the Uxia app for your workspace first. Members will see a **Disabled by admin** label until it's turned on — admins can enable Uxia from **Workspace settings → Apps**.
</Note>

## Usage

Once connected, you can ask ChatGPT to talk to Uxia directly:

```plaintext
Create a new test that tests my new app www.google.com
```

```plaintext
List my last 5 Uxia tests and summarize the top insights from each.
```
