Setting up an Accessibility Test starts the same way as any Uxia test, then asks you to pick a WCAG target level before you upload anything.

## Creating the test

Start from **Home → Create test** or **Tests → Create test**. In the **Create new test** modal, choose **Accessibility Test** from the three test types, give it a clear title (optionally assign it to a project), and click **Create test**. Uxia opens the Accessibility Test wizard, which runs through three steps: **Test Details**, **Upload Frames**, and **Summary**.

## Choosing the conformance level

On the **Test Details** step, you pick the WCAG target level:

- **Recommended (AA)** — preselected, and the right choice for most teams. It addresses the most common accessibility barriers and is positioned as the industry and legal baseline.
- **Advanced (AAA)** — the highest level of coverage, best when you're aiming for the most inclusive experience or working to a stricter internal standard.

<Note>
  WCAG levels are cumulative. To pass **AA**, a product must also satisfy **A**; to pass **AAA**, it must satisfy **A**, **AA**, and **AAA**. Leave AA selected unless your team explicitly needs AAA.
</Note>

## How accessibility testing works

The expandable **How accessibility testing works** section explains what Uxia evaluates:

- WCAG 2.2 conformance (AA / AAA)
- Low-vision support
- Mobile and touch interactions
- Cognitive accessibility

It also includes FAQ-style entries on legal standards — the European Accessibility Act (EAA), the ADA, and other countries — whether WCAG 2.2 still applies when a law references 2.1, whether the test covers code-level issues, and how the A / AA / AAA levels relate.

Confirm your title, leave AA selected unless you need AAA, then click **Next** to move on to uploading frames.
