The **Analytics** tab is the behavior-focused screen. Where the [Insights Report](/results) tells you what the problems are, Analytics shows how testers moved through the flow and where friction actually happened. Everything here is derived from the testers' step-by-step transcripts.

## Navigation overview

The top section summarizes movement through the flow:

| Metric | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Total testers** | The planned tester count for the test — the denominator for the rates below |
| **Success rate** | Share of testers who reached the goal |
| **Drop-off rate** | Share who left before completing (the inverse of success rate) |
| **Misclick rate** | Share of clicks that didn't do what a click should |
| **Average steps** | Typical number of steps taken to complete |
| **Min / Max steps** | The shortest and longest paths taken |

### How the numbers are calculated

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Success rate & drop-off">
    A tester counts as **successful** when they reach the goal:

    - On **live tests**, when any step reaches the stop condition or marks the mission complete.
    - On **interactive prototypes** with a defined end frame, only when their last real step lands on that end frame.
    - Otherwise, when they reach a final step.

    A tester who drops off at any step counts as unsuccessful. **Success rate** is successful testers ÷ total testers. **Drop-off rate** isn't measured separately — it's 100% minus the success rate.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Misclick rate">
    A misclick is a click that didn't behave like a successful click. How it's measured depends on the source:

    - **Interactive prototypes** — of the clicks that could be matched to an interactive target, the share that missed one (landed outside a hotspot).
    - **Live prototype and slideshow flows** — of the click-like actions (waits excluded), the share after which the screen didn't change.
    - **Plain live-website tests** — not available, shown as a dash.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Step counts">
    Steps are counted as **logical steps**, not raw actions: the final reflection step is excluded, and consecutive scrolls in the same direction collapse into a single step. Average, minimum, and maximum are then taken across all testers.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Journeys diagram

Below the overview, a **Journeys Diagram** lays out the step paths with frame previews, so you can see how testers progressed from screen to screen — and where paths split, loop, or diverge between successful and unsuccessful runs. Consecutive repeats of the same frame are collapsed first, then the diagram shows:

- **Nodes** — each node is a frame at a position in the journey. Its count is the number of unique testers who were on that frame at that point; its percentage is that count over all testers in the journey.
- **Edges** — the number of unique testers who moved from one frame to the next.

### Paths

Testers are also grouped by the exact sequence of frames they visited (for example, `0 > 2 > 4`):

| Column | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Status** | **Success** only if every tester on the path completed the flow; otherwise **Drop-off** |
| **Testers** | Unique testers who followed that exact path |
| **Steps** | Number of frames visited in the path (not raw clicks) |
| **Misclicks** | The misclick rate above, scoped to the testers on that path |

## Frame analytics

Select any frame to drill into how testers behaved on that specific screen:

| View | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Heatmap** | Where testers clicked on the frame |
| **Total testers** | Testers who had at least one real step on the frame |
| **Total clicks** | Evaluable clicks on the frame — not every raw action |
| **Misclick rate** | The misclick rate above, scoped to this screen |
| **Average attempts** | Typical number of tries per visit to the screen |
| **Tester comments** | Remarks tied to that specific frame |

<Note>
  **Average attempts** is the mean number of action attempts per visit to a screen; on live prototype flows it's shown instead as total clicks divided by the testers who reached the screen. Per-screen **misclick rate** is hidden (shown as a dash) when a screen has no recorded clicks or no interactive targets, and for plain live-website tests.
</Note>

This is the best tab for questions like: where do testers hesitate, which screen causes misclicks, how many steps success usually takes, and where successful and unsuccessful paths part ways.
