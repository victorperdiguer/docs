The **Analytics** tab is the behavior-focused screen. Where the [Insights Report](/results) tells you what the problems are, Analytics shows how testers moved through the flow and where friction actually happened.

## Navigation overview

The top section summarizes movement through the flow:

| Metric | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Total testers** | How many synthetic testers ran the flow |
| **Success rate** | Share of testers who reached the goal |
| **Drop-off rate** | Share who left before completing |
| **Misclick rate** | How often testers clicked somewhere that didn't move them forward |
| **Average steps** | Typical number of steps taken to complete |
| **Min / Max steps** | The shortest and longest paths taken |

## Journeys diagram

Below the overview, a **Journeys Diagram** lays out the step paths with frame previews, so you can see how testers progressed from screen to screen — and where paths split, loop, or diverge between successful and unsuccessful runs.

## Frame analytics

Select any frame to drill into how testers behaved on that specific screen:

| View | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Heatmap** | Where testers clicked on the frame |
| **Total clicks** | How many clicks the frame received |
| **Misclick rate** | How often clicks on this frame didn't lead where the tester expected |
| **Average attempts** | Typical number of tries before moving on |
| **Tester comments** | Remarks tied to that specific frame |

This is the best tab for questions like: where do testers hesitate, which screen causes misclicks, how many steps success usually takes, and where successful and unsuccessful paths part ways.
