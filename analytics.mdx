Alongside the qualitative findings, every report scores your product against three standardized UX benchmarks — a consistent, comparable measure you can track over time and put in front of stakeholders.

## The three scores

- **SUS (System Usability Scale)** — a single number from 0–100, drawn from 10 attitudinal questions. 68 is the empirical average across thousands of products; above 68 is good, above 80 is excellent, and below 68 signals real usability debt.
- **SUPR-Q** — a broader read expressed as a percentile rank across four dimensions: Usability, Trust, Loyalty, and Appearance. It's how you spot _which_ dimension is dragging the experience down.
- **Uxia Optimization Score** — Uxia's proprietary benchmark, scored against others in your specific industry vertical rather than a universal dataset, so it tells you whether your UX is actually competitive in your category.

## Getting value from them

Scores earn their keep when you use them consistently:

- **Track improvement** — run a test before and after a redesign; a rising SUS confirms the change actually helped, not just that it looked better in review.
- **Make the case for UX work** — a below-average score is a defensible, quantified argument for investment.
- **Prioritize by severity** — pair the scores with the Insights Report to stack-rank fixes, tackling whatever's pulling you below the 68 SUS line first.
- **Compare product areas** — test different flows separately to find where you're weakest.
