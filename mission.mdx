title: "Mission"
---

The **Tester Mission** is a short, clear instruction describing what you want the AI user to achieve. It's the single most important field in the builder — it defines the goal of the session and keeps every tester's feedback focused and actionable. The mission is required, and the more specific and granular you make it, the better your results.

## Write it like a single concrete task

Good missions describe one goal with a clear end state:

- "Complete the registration flow using a social login."
- "Upgrade your plan from free to premium using a credit card."
- "Find and purchase one ticket for tonight's 9:00 PM showing."

## Tips for effective missions

<Tip>
  **Lead with the goal, not the steps.** Write "Find a plan that fits your budget and sign up" rather than "Click Pricing, select Pro, then fill in your details." Step-by-step instructions train testers to follow directions instead of revealing where your design actually fails.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Avoid leading language.** Don't name UI elements ("click the blue button"), imply a correct path ("find the easy way to…"), or signal what testers should feel ("notice how simple it is…"). Neutral wording is everything.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **One mission, one primary goal.** If you need to test several flows, run several tests. Compound missions produce muddled results.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Each field has a **Review** helper that tightens your wording for clarity. Some flows aren't supported by AI participants — if a mission depends on one, you'll see a prompt to learn more.
</Note>
