Connect Figma to turn designs into actionable prototypes in Uxia. Sync projects and keep product, design, and development aligned.

## Native Figma integration

Uxia's native Figma integration lets you import your built Figma prototypes directly into Uxia in one click — no exporting, screenshotting, or rebuilding flows. Once imported, your prototype is fully editable inside the Uxia canvas:

- Rearrange screens and update flow connections
- Edit, add, or remove interactions and hotspots
- Adjust starting points and task entry screens
- Make on-the-fly changes between test runs without going back to Figma

This means you can refine the prototype based on AI tester feedback and re-run tests immediately, keeping your research loop tight.

## Where the Figma integration is available

The Figma import flow is available when you are creating or editing a **prototype-based** test in Uxia.

To access it:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the test">
    Open the test you want to import a Figma prototype into.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Prototype step">
    Navigate to the **Prototype** step of the test builder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Interactive flow">
    Choose **Interactive flow** as the prototype type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the import dialog">
    Click **Import designs** on an empty canvas, or **Import Files** if the canvas already has frames.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Figma import is available for **Interactive flow** only. If **Sequential flow** is selected, the Figma option is disabled.
</Note>

## Import a Figma prototype

Once the **Import files** modal opens:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Figma">
    Choose **Figma** as the source.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm fetch flows">
    Confirm the card labeled **Fetch flows from Figma**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your account">
    Click **OAuth with Figma** to connect your Figma account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your Figma URL">
    Paste a Figma **proto**, **file**, or **design** URL into the **Figma URL** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for flow detection">
    Wait for Uxia to detect the available flows in your file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a flow">
    In **Select a flow**, choose the flow you want to import.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Uxia then imports the screens from that flow into the prototype canvas, where you can edit them freely before launching your test.

## Tips and best practices

- Use one Figma file per flow when possible — it makes imports cleaner and easier to review.
- Make sure your Figma prototype has a **Flow starting point** set, or Uxia may not detect any flows.
- Keep each imported flow focused and within your frame limit.
- Use a real prototype flow, not a loose design file with no clickable path.
- If your browser blocks pop-ups, allow them so the Figma OAuth window can open.
- If you already have frames on the canvas, importing a Figma flow will replace the existing frames.
- Larger Figma files can take longer to load, so simpler files generally import faster.
