---
title: "Use Cases"
---

An AI User Test earns its place whenever speed, reach, or preparation matters more than having a human physically in the loop. Three situations where it's particularly useful:

**Fast iteration cycles.** When you need signal quickly and can't afford the recruit-schedule-run cycle that human testing requires. Synthetic testers start immediately and finish in minutes, so you can test, fix, and re-test several times in the time a single round of human recruitment would take.

**Hard-to-reach audiences.** When your target users are niche, specialized, or simply hard to get hold of — the kind of panel that's slow or expensive to recruit — you replicate them with [synthetic testers](/concepts/ai-participants) built to match the profile you're after, rather than waiting on people you can't easily reach.

**Polishing before human testing.** When you do want real people in the loop, run synthetic testing first to catch the obvious problems and tighten the flows. Your human sessions — which cost more time and money — are then spent on the subtle, high-value questions instead of issues you could have caught earlier. You can turn any AI User Test into a [Human Test](/testing/human-test) on the same setup.

<Note>
  **Where it's not the right fit:** AI User Tests aren't suited to flows where animation, fluidity, or dynamism carries the experience. Synthetic testers don't capture motion and micro-interactions the way a person would, so if that's the core of what you're validating, reach for human testing instead.
</Note>
