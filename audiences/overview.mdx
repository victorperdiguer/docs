An **audience** defines who tests your product. It's a reusable set of criteria that Uxia turns into a panel of synthetic AI testers — each one an AI-generated participant that navigates, reacts, and thinks aloud the way a real person matching that audience would. You get usable feedback in minutes, without recruiting or scheduling humans. Build an audience once from the **Audiences** area or inline while setting up a test, then reuse it across as many tests as you like.

## The three layers of an audience

Every Uxia audience is built on three layers that stack into a complete, believable person.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Sociodemographic">
    The foundation — age, gender, languages, country and nationality, education, employment, industry, role, income, and more.

    You set only what matters to you; whatever you leave blank, Uxia completes using publicly available data sources so each tester stays statistically representative of the population they belong to, rather than filled with random values.

    On a tester's profile, the fields you set appear as **Defined Filters** and the ones Uxia generated as **Auto-Populated Filters**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Psychological">
    Every tester carries a personality based on the **OCEAN model** (the Big Five) — Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism — one of the most established frameworks in psychology for describing personality and predicting behavior.

    It's shown as a radar chart and shapes how a tester approaches tasks, reacts to friction, and phrases feedback.

    This is where you tune the _temperament_ of your panel: test with users who are easily frustrated or highly patient, more or less empathetic, cautious or exploratory.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Enrichment">
    An optional custom layer that uses your own data — interviews, personas, research — to generate testers with far higher fidelity to your real users. See [Audience Enrichment](/account/audiences/audience-enrichment).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## What makes Uxia's testers different

Uxia's synthetic testers aren't generic language-model output. Each is grounded in proprietary behavioral and psychographic datasets that model how people with specific backgrounds and motivations actually use digital products. So a 22-year-old student with low tech literacy doesn't just _say_ different things than a 45-year-old IT manager — they _behave_ differently, hesitating in different places, trusting different signals, and reading UI through different mental models.

During a test, each tester works through your product while **thinking aloud** — narrating what they expect, calling out confusion or surprise, and explaining why they did or didn't click something. That narration is captured as a full transcript, giving you the reasoning behind every interaction rather than just the outcome.
