This page covers the mechanics of creating an audience and choosing your testers. For the concepts behind it — the three layers and how synthetic testers work — see the [Overview](/account/audiences).

## Where audiences live

Audiences are reusable, so you can build one once and use it across tests. Each is shown as a card with its name, tester count, age range, and gender mix, plus a menu to **Select Testers**, **Review filters**, **Rename**, **Clone**, or **Delete**. Cloning copies the criteria but not the generated testers — handy when you want a variation on an existing segment.

## Setting up filters

In the builder, give the audience a name, then switch filters on from the picker on the right; active filters collect on the left. New audiences start with the three core filters already active:

- **Age** — a range anywhere from 18 to 100
- **Gender** — male, female, or both
- **Languages** — defaults to English, with the option to match any or all selected languages

Beyond those, a wider set lets you tighten the segment: country of residence and nationality, tech literacy, education, marital and employment status, industry, role, and household or personal income. There's also a free-text **Custom Details** field for lifestyle, motivations, routines, or other traits. You don't need to fill everything in — anything you leave blank is auto-populated to keep each tester coherent.

<Note>
  Advanced filters and Quick Fill are gated by plan. On lower tiers you'll see an **Unlock more audience filters** prompt where they'd otherwise appear.
</Note>

### Quick Fill

If you'd rather not set filters by hand, **Quick Fill** (top right) does it for you in one of two ways:

- **Smart URL** — paste a product or website URL and Uxia scrapes the page, infers who the product is for, and proposes a matching set of filters for you to review and adjust.
- **Manual** — describe your target users in free text (an audience blueprint) and let Uxia translate that into filters.

If you continue with no filters set, Uxia warns that it will generate random testers.

<Warning>
  Once you generate testers, the audience's filters and details are **locked**. To change them, create a new audience — cloning an existing one is the quickest way to reuse the same criteria.
</Warning>

## Generating and selecting testers

After you save the audience, Uxia takes you to tester selection and starts generating profiles that match your criteria. If none exist yet it auto-generates a first batch, and **Generate more testers** widens the pool whenever you want more choices.

Each tester appears as a card with two views:

- a **demographic / lifestyle** view — nationality, country, languages, tech literacy, education, industry, role, employment, income, and a short description, with Defined and Auto-Populated attributes marked by their badges
- a **psychological profile** view — the OCEAN radar chart plus a narrative of the tester's motivations and decision-making style

Testers built with enrichment carry an **Enriched** badge. If one isn't quite right, **Replace tester** swaps it for a fresh profile that still matches all your criteria.

Click a card to select it. A sticky progress bar tracks your selection (`x / y selected`) and flags when you've hit the minimum or fully allocated your slots; the cap depends on your plan. **Review Audience Filters** opens a side panel where you can revisit the filters and search, review, or edit the tester list before launching.
