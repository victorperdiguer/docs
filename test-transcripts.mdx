title: "Test Transcripts"
---

Every synthetic tester produces a complete transcript of their session — not a log of clicks and page views, but a running narration of the experience as it happened.

## What a transcript contains

Each transcript captures the full think-aloud stream from a single tester:

- **Actions taken** — what the tester clicked, scrolled past, ignored, or interacted with, in sequence
- **Reasoning** — why they made each decision, in their own words
- **Hesitation moments** — where they paused, second-guessed, or re-read something
- **Confusion and frustration** — explicit moments where something didn't work as expected or clashed with their mental model
- **Expectations** — what they anticipated would happen, and whether reality matched

## How to read them

Transcripts are shown per tester, so you can follow one person's complete journey from start to finish. That individual view is where you catch patterns unique to a user type — a low-tech-literacy tester struggling at a step that high-literacy testers sailed through, for instance.

Then look across transcripts for recurring friction: if three of five testers hesitate at the same screen, that's a signal worth acting on regardless of what the summary says.

<Tip>
  Don't read only the Insights Report and skip the transcripts. The report tells you what the problems are; the transcripts tell you _how_ users experience them — and that texture is what separates a real fix from a patch that misses the root cause.
</Tip>
