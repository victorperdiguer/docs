The **Test Transcripts** tab shows each participant's journey step by step, in transcript form — the raw reasoning behind the summarized findings rather than the synthesis itself.

## What each step shows

A transcript reads like a narrated walkthrough. Every step captures:

| Element | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Participant** | Who the tester is |
| **Completion status** | Whether they finished the flow |
| **Step number** | Where they are in the journey |
| **Frame** | The screen at that step |
| **Action** | What they did — a click, a text input, and so on |
| **Reasoning** | Why they did it, in first-person language |

Each step also has a **Show more details** action for a closer look.

## How to read them

Because the reasoning is in the tester's own words, this is where you understand the _why_ behind a finding. You can watch a tester explain why they tap **Next**, the moment onboarding starts to feel too long, why they pick the 1-hour ticket, or when wording like "invoice" turns confusing.

Reach for this tab when you want raw reasoning, not just summarized results — and read it alongside the [Insights Report](/results), which tells you which of these moments recurred across the panel.
