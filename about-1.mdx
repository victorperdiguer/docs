title: "About"
---

The Accessibility Test audits your product designs against [WCAG 2.2](/wcag-conformance-level) before you write code. Upload your screens, pick a target conformance level, and Uxia checks them criterion by criterion — flagging what passes, what fails, and what needs a human review, with each issue tagged as a likely design or code fix.

Because it runs on static screens and recordings rather than a live build, you catch accessibility problems at the design stage — where they're cheap to fix — instead of late in QA or after launch.

What it covers:

- **WCAG 2.2 conformance** at AA or AAA (the levels are cumulative)
- **Low-vision support**, including a [vision simulation](/vision-simulation) view
- **Mobile and touch interactions**
- **Cognitive accessibility**

You get a shareable report split into criteria results, vision simulation, and the audit record — see [Criteria Evaluation](/criteria-evaluation) for how to read it.

<Note>
  Uxia helps you catch issues early, but it doesn't replace a formal legal accessibility audit, specialist review, or certification.
</Note>
