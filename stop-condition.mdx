title: "Stop Condition"
---

The **Stop Condition** defines exactly when a run should end — the moment a tester knows the goal has been reached. It's required for [live sources](/source), because a tester navigating a real, open-ended website needs a clear signal for when to stop rather than wandering indefinitely.

## Writing a stop condition

State the end state in plain language, tied to something observable on screen. For example:

> "Stop once I have completed checkout and reached the order confirmation page."

> "Stop when the new account has been created and I land on the dashboard."

A good stop condition pairs naturally with a good [mission](/mission): the mission says what to accomplish, and the stop condition says how you'll know it's done. Anchoring it to a specific page, confirmation message, or state change keeps runs clean and comparable across testers.

<Tip>
  If testers are stopping too early or running long, tighten the stop condition before anything else — it's usually the fastest fix for inconsistent runs.
</Tip>
