When a test finishes, Uxia gives you results in two layers: full think-aloud [transcripts](/test-transcripts) from each individual tester, and an auto-generated **Insights Report** that synthesizes findings across the whole session. Together they tell you both _why_ something is broken and _what_ to fix first — with no tagging, coding, or manual analysis on your part.

## The Insights Report

The report is ready as soon as the last tester finishes. It includes:

- **UX friction summary** — a plain-language overview of the most significant usability issues found across all testers, with context on where and how they surfaced.
- **Categorized issues** — findings grouped into Usability, Navigation, Copy & Messaging, and Trust & Credibility, so you can route them to the right people.
- **Prioritized recommendations** — each issue comes with an actionable fix, ordered by the severity and frequency of the friction observed.
- **Standardized metrics** — SUS, SUPR-Q equivalents, and the Uxia Optimization Score. See [Analytics](/analytics) for how to read them.

## Sharing

Reports are shareable via a link — no Uxia account required to view one. That makes it easy to drop findings into a team review, hand them to a developer, or include them in a stakeholder deck.
