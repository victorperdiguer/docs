title: "Test Set-up Confidence"
---

A test is only as good as its setup — a vague mission or a missing stop condition quietly weakens every result that follows. Uxia surfaces a few signals while you build, so you can catch these before launch rather than after.

## Where confidence comes from

- **Review helpers** on the [mission](/mission), [scenario](/test-scenario), and post-test questions refine your wording for clarity, nudging you toward instructions testers can actually act on.
- **Unsupported-flow prompts** — if a mission depends on something AI participants can't do, you're flagged before launch instead of discovering it in the results.
- **The Test Summary** recaps every section — source, mission, scenario, stop condition, questions, audience, and advanced settings — each with an Edit button, so nothing ships half-configured.

The stronger and more specific each of these is, the more you can trust the findings that come back. When you're unsure where to start, tighten the [mission](/mission) and [stop condition](/stop-condition) first — they carry the most weight.
