If you added [post-test questions](/post-test-questions) when building the test, this is where you read the answers back. Every tester's response to each question appears alongside the rest of your results, so you can see how the panel reflected on the flow once they'd finished it.

## Reading the answers

Post-test answers are most useful read against the transcripts rather than on their own. A tester might complete the flow and still report low confidence — the answer flags it, and the [transcript](/test-transcripts) shows you the moment that shook them.

Look for:

- **Agreement across testers** — several people naming the same friction point turns a hunch into a finding.
- **Gaps between behavior and perception** — testers who succeeded but felt unsure, or struggled but rated it easy, both point to something worth a closer look.
- **Specific, actionable language** — open-ended answers often hand you the exact wording of what confused people.
