title: "Vision Simulation"
---

The **Vision Simulation** tab is a visual comparison tool: it renders the same frame under different visual conditions so you can check whether your design still holds up when perception changes.

## Reading the comparison

The tab shows side-by-side preview panels — a baseline view such as **Normal Vision** next to a simulated view such as **Red-Blind** — with condition selectors and a severity slider (observed at 50%). Switching conditions shows you:

- Whether your color choices still communicate what they need to
- Whether visual hierarchy and emphasis survive when color perception shifts

It's the fastest way to catch cases where meaning is carried by color alone, or where contrast quietly collapses for a segment of users.

## Test Details tab

The third tab, **Test Details**, is the record of the run. It surfaces the conformance level and its rationale, any tags (the section shows even when empty), the uploaded frames as thumbnails, and the **How accessibility testing works** explainer again for later reference.
