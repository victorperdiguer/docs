A Human Test bridges the speed of AI testing with the nuance of real user feedback. Once you've run an AI test in Uxia, you can generate a shareable link that sends your own participants through the exact same mission, scenario, and prototype. Their recordings, transcripts, and interaction data land alongside your synthetic results — giving you a unified view of how both AI and human testers experienced your product.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run an AI test first">
    Start with an [AI User Test](/testing/ai-user-test) or [AI Live Website Test](/testing/ai-live-website). Define your mission, scenario, and prototype. Once the AI test is set up, you're ready to extend it to human participants.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate your shareable link">
    From your test dashboard, create a Human Test link. Uxia uses the same mission and scenario from your AI test — no duplicate setup required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the link to your participants">
    Share the link with your own users, customers, recruited participants, or panel members via email, Slack, social media, or any channel you use to reach them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Participants complete the test">
    Each participant opens the link, reads the mission and scenario, grants recording permissions, and completes the test independently — no moderator or scheduling needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review combined AI and human results">
    Human session data appears in the same Uxia report as your synthetic results. Compare AI and human findings side by side — including SUS scores, the UXIA-Q Score, the Usability Optimization Score, and per-tester transcripts — to identify where synthetic and human responses align and where they diverge. Download or share the combined insights report with your team or stakeholders.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What gets captured

Every human test session records a rich set of data automatically:

- **Screen recording** — a full video of everything the participant saw and did during the session
- **Face recording** _(optional)_ — captures facial expressions and emotional reactions via webcam
- **Microphone audio** — records spoken commentary and natural reactions throughout the session
- **Click and interaction data** — every tap, click, scroll, and input is logged
- **Heatmaps** — visualize where participants focused, clicked, and hovered across each screen
- **Auto-generated transcripts** — every session is transcribed automatically, so you can search, skim, and quote without rewatching video

## The participant experience

Participants don't need an account or any prior knowledge of Uxia. When they open your link, they:

1. Read the mission and scenario — the same context your AI testers received
2. Review and agree to recording permissions (screen, face, and microphone)
3. Complete the test at their own pace, interacting with your prototype or live product naturally
4. Submit when they're done — the session is processed and available in your dashboard automatically

The experience is straightforward and low-friction, so participants can focus entirely on the task rather than the tooling.

## Participant sourcing

<Note>
  Uxia does not recruit participants on your behalf. You bring your own — whether that's existing customers, internal users, a panel you manage, or participants you've recruited through another channel. This keeps you in control of who provides feedback and ensures your results reflect the audience that matters most to your product.
</Note>

## Why combine AI and human testing

<Tip>
  AI tests give you results in minutes and let you run as many rounds as you need. Human tests add the emotional depth, lived context, and unpredictable behavior that only real users bring. Running both gives you the best of both worlds: use AI to iterate fast and narrow down issues, then use human sessions to confirm findings, surface edge cases, and build stakeholder confidence in your decisions.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to run an AI test before creating a Human Test?">
    Yes. A Human Test is built on top of an existing AI test — it reuses the same mission, scenario, and prototype configuration. This ensures your AI and human results are directly comparable, since every tester followed the same brief.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can participants use any device?">
    Human Tests are designed to work in a standard web browser, so participants can join from desktop or laptop. Screen and microphone capture are handled through the browser without any downloads. Face recording via webcam is optional and requires webcam access.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How are transcripts generated?">
    Transcripts are generated automatically from the microphone audio captured during each session. They're available in your dashboard as soon as the session is processed, typically within a few minutes of completion. You can read, search, and quote from transcripts without watching the full recording.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How many human participants should I include?">
    There's no fixed requirement — the right number depends on your goals. For usability testing, five to eight participants typically surface the most actionable issues. If you're comparing segments or building statistical confidence, you may want more. Uxia lets you send your link to as many participants as you need and review results as sessions come in.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
