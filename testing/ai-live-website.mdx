An AI Live Website Test lets you point Uxia at any URL and get immediate, in-depth UX feedback — no prototype setup, no participant recruitment, no waiting. Synthetic testers visit your page exactly as a real user would, navigate freely, and surface usability issues, confusion points, and opportunities for improvement.

## What you can test

The Live Website Test works with anything accessible via a URL:

- **Live websites** — production sites and landing pages
- **Figma prototypes** — share your prototype link directly
- **Staging and pre-production environments** — test before you ship
- **Partially built products** — get feedback on works in progress
- **Gated and authenticated areas** — provide login credentials to test post-login flows

## How to set up a live website test

<Steps>
  <Step title="Paste your URL">
    Enter the URL you want to test. This can be a live site, a staging environment, a Figma prototype link, or any other accessible address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define your audience">
    Configure the profile of your synthetic testers — their demographics (age, location, occupation), psychographic attributes (tech-savviness, domain familiarity), or upload a persona file to match your real users. The right audience profile produces feedback that reflects your actual target market.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your mission">
    Write the goal you want testers to accomplish. A clear mission produces focused, actionable results. For example: _"Find and purchase a pair of running shoes under \$100."_
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the test">
    Send your testers to the URL. They'll navigate independently, think aloud, and interact with your product just as real users would.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and share your report">
    Once testing is complete, Uxia delivers a full report with friction points, behavioral patterns, transcripts, and standardized usability metrics. Download a shareable insights report to distribute findings across your team or to stakeholders.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you get

Your report includes the same comprehensive output as an AI User Test:

- **UX friction summary** — the most impactful barriers across all tester sessions
- **Usability issues and confusion points** — specific moments where testers struggled or abandoned tasks
- **Behavioral patterns** — trends in how testers navigated, where they paused, and what they ignored
- **Opportunities for improvement** — design and copy changes most likely to improve outcomes
- **Standardized metrics** — SUS (System Usability Scale) scores, the UXIA-Q Score, and the Usability Optimization Score
- **Industry benchmarks** — compare your results against category baselines

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I test a site that requires a login?">
    Yes. When configuring your test, you can supply credentials for password-protected or authenticated areas. Synthetic testers will log in using those details, so you can test gated flows like onboarding, account settings, or checkout without exposing anything publicly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the difference between a Live Website Test and an AI User Test?">
    Both use the same synthetic testers and produce the same style of report. The distinction is in how you approach setup. A Live Website Test is optimized for anything accessible via a URL — paste a link and go. An AI User Test offers the same coverage and additionally supports uploading static design files and structured flows that may not yet have a live URL.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I test a site that isn't finished yet?">
    Yes. Synthetic testers can navigate partially built products and provide useful feedback even when not every feature is complete. Just give testers a mission that's achievable within your current build, and Uxia will surface friction within the scope of what's available.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How quickly do I get results?">
    Most live website tests complete within minutes. The exact time depends on the complexity of the mission and the number of testers you configure. You'll be notified when your report is ready.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
